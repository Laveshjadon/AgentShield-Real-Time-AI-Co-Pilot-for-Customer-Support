ESCALATION AND COMPLAINT HANDLING GUIDE
=========================================

1. When to Escalate to Supervisor
Escalate the call to your supervisor immediately if:
- The customer explicitly asks to speak to a supervisor or manager
- The customer uses threatening or abusive language repeatedly
- The agent has tried all troubleshooting steps and the issue is unresolved
- The customer is threatening legal action or filing a consumer forum complaint
- The issue involves a refund above Rs 10,000
- The customer is a VIP or business account holder

2. How to Put a Customer on Hold
Before putting a customer on hold:
- Always ask permission: "May I please put you on hold for 1-2 minutes while I check this?"
- Never hold a customer for more than 3 minutes without checking back
- If you need more time, check back and inform the customer: "I need 2 more minutes, is that okay?"
- Never abandon a customer on hold

3. Handling Angry Customers
Step 1: Let the customer speak without interrupting
Step 2: Acknowledge their frustration: "I completely understand your frustration and I sincerely apologize for the inconvenience."
Step 3: Do NOT argue or become defensive
Step 4: Focus on what you CAN do, not what you cannot
Step 5: Offer a concrete next step with a timeline: "I will personally ensure this is resolved by tomorrow 5 PM."
Step 6: If the customer calms down, thank them for their patience

4. Handling Abusive Language
If a customer uses abusive language:
- Warn calmly: "Sir/Madam, I want to help you but I need us to have a respectful conversation."
- If abuse continues: "I understand you are upset. If the language continues, I will need to end this call."
- If abuse continues after the warning: End the call professionally and log the incident in CRM
- Do not respond to abuse with anger or sarcasm

5. Consumer Forum / Legal Threats
If a customer threatens to go to consumer forum or file a legal case:
- Do not panic or argue
- Say: "I understand your concern. I would like to resolve this for you before it reaches that stage."
- Immediately escalate to your supervisor
- Note the exact words the customer used in the call log
- Do not make any promises you are not authorized to make

6. Complaint Registration
Every complaint must be registered in the CRM system:
- Complaint ID must be shared with the customer before ending the call
- Expected resolution time must be communicated clearly
- Standard resolution time: 24 hours for billing, 48 hours for technical, 7 days for refunds
- After the complaint is resolved, call the customer back to confirm satisfaction

7. Difficult Call De-escalation Phrases
Use these phrases to calm an angry customer:
- "I completely understand how frustrating this must be for you."
- "You are absolutely right to feel this way."
- "I am going to personally take ownership of this issue."
- "Let me see exactly what I can do for you right now."
- "I appreciate your patience, and I will make sure this is sorted out."
