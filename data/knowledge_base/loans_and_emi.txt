LOANS & EMI POLICY

Q: Customer's EMI was deducted but loan account not updated.
A: EMI deduction and loan account update run on separate systems. Updates reflect within 24 hours of deduction. If still not updated after 24 hours, verify payment receipt and raise a reconciliation ticket.

Q: Customer wants to pre-close their loan.
A: Pre-closure steps:
1. Customer must complete a minimum of 6 EMIs before pre-closure is allowed.
2. Pre-closure charge: 2% of outstanding principal (waived for floating rate loans after RBI guidelines).
3. Customer must request pre-closure in writing (email or branch visit).
4. Final outstanding amount will be calculated on the date of pre-closure.
5. NOC (No Objection Certificate) issued within 15 business days after full payment.

Q: Customer missed an EMI — what are the consequences?
A: Late payment charges: ₹500 or 2% of EMI amount, whichever is higher, per missed EMI. Credit score impact: reported to CIBIL if EMI is overdue by more than 30 days. Offer the customer a one-time EMI restructuring option if they are facing genuine financial difficulty.

Q: Customer asking about loan eligibility.
A: Eligibility criteria:
- Salaried: minimum net salary ₹25,000/month, minimum 6 months in current job
- Self-employed: minimum 2 years of business, ITR for last 2 years required
- CIBIL score: minimum 700 for personal loans, 650 for secured loans
- Age: 21–60 years (salaried), 21–65 years (self-employed)
Direct customer to apply online or visit branch for final eligibility check.

Q: What documents are needed for a personal loan?
A: Required documents:
- PAN card (mandatory)
- Aadhaar card
- Last 3 months salary slips (salaried) or last 2 years ITR (self-employed)
- Last 6 months bank statement
- Address proof (utility bill, passport, or rental agreement)

FIXED DEPOSITS

Q: How to open a Fixed Deposit?
A: FDs can be opened via app, net banking, or branch. Minimum amount: ₹1,000. Tenure: 7 days to 10 years. Interest rates vary by tenure — current rates available on website and app. Senior citizens get 0.5% additional interest.

Q: Customer wants to break FD prematurely.
A: Premature withdrawal allowed with 1% penalty on the contracted rate. FDs under 7 days cannot be broken prematurely. Loan against FD available up to 90% of FD value as an alternative to breaking it.
